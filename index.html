I am a Provost Postdoctoral Scholar in the Humanities in the School of Philosophy at USC. I work mostly in the philosophy of mind and language, metaphysics, and the philosophy of mathematics. I am also interested in some questions in metaethics, formal epistemology, and the philosophy of science.


Before coming to USC, I was a graduate student in the Deparment of Linguistics and Philosophy at MIT, where I received my PhD in 2011. Before that, I spent some time in Paris, studying musicology, philosophy, and logic.
